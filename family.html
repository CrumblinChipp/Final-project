Family Members

Our parents were raised on the poor side of society. Mother has 12 siblings, and her parents were old enough to have experienced the Japanese colonization. Father's side is divided by religion, leading to both of them being conservatives. My running theory is that my parents' minds are woven around where almost anything still runs its course around ignorance and plain black and white, whereas my generation's minds were built around making everything make sense.

There are things we cannot talk about within the family, especially with our parents. Showing affection isn't a big thing in our family, and neither is apologizing; we ignore each other until the fire of the argument burns down. Our parents are religious people, unlike us siblings. Unfortunately, we learned to question things and make sense of everything, leading to religion not making sense to us.

Our family is close, at least that is how our extended family describes it. They say we're just like a group of friends, able to speak casually with each other and joke around. I do agree that we have quite a bond when it comes to gathering and having fun. However, there is always something hidden when it comes to a happy family.
